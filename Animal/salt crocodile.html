Salt Crocodile

Appearance

The saltwater crocodile (Crocodylus porosus), also known as the "salty" or "Indo-Pacific crocodile," is the largest living crocodile species and is well-adapted to a wide range of habitats in the Indo-Pacific region.Reaching lengths of more than 23 feet (6.5 m) and weights over 2,200 pounds the saltwater crocodile is the largest reptile on the planet and is a formidable predator throughout its range. Saltwater crocodiles of this size are capable of eating just about any animal that strays too close and are particularly adept at drowning terrestrial creatures like birds and mammals. Named for its ability to survive in full salinity seawater, saltwater crocodiles typically live in brackish (low salinity) water near the coast.

Habitat

Saltwater crocodile inhabits coastal brackish mangrove swamps, river deltas, and freshwater rivers.Though they have few natural predators, saltwater crocodiles have been hunted for many decades by people. Their eggs and meat are eaten, and their skin is particularly valuable for use as a material for bags, shoes, and other goods. Distribution

It is one of the most widely distributed of all crocodilians, ranging from southern India and Sri Lanka, throughout southeast Asia, east through the Philippines to Micronesia, and down through Indonesia, Papua New Guinea and the Solomon Islands to northern Australia.

Let`s Explore The Hidden Gems

Saltwater crocodiles have incredibly strong jaws capable of crushing almost anything they can fit in their mouths. However, despite this brute strength, they can be quite picky eaters, often snubbing perfectly good meals if they're not in the mood for it.

: Despite being fearsome predators, saltwater crocodiles can be surprisingly lazy. They're known to spend much of their time basking in the sun, resembling sunbathers lounging by the poolside. Maybe they're just waiting for their next meal to swim by!

Saltwater crocodiles have a distinct fashion sense - well, sort of. Their rugged, armored skin might seem like a fashion statement, but it's actually an evolutionary adaptation for protection. Saltwater crocodiles are masters of social distancing. They prefer to live solitary lives, avoiding crowded places like the plague (pun intended!). So, if you ever spot one in the wild, don't expect it to be throwing a wild party with its crocodile buddies - they're more likely to be enjoying some quality alone time.

Where to Find?...

Neyyar Dam

Neyyar Dam and its surrounding area in Kerala is one of the places where saltwater crocodiles have been sighted. Neyyar Dam is located in Thiruvananthapuram district of Kerala. The Neyyar Wildlife Sanctuary, which surrounds the Neyyar Dam reservoir, is known to be home to a variety of wildlife, including saltwater crocodiles.Within the Neyyar Wildlife Sanctuary, saltwater crocodiles can be found in the Neyyar reservoir and its adjoining areas, which include marshes, mangrove forests, and riverine habitats. These crocodiles are often spotted basking along the banks of the reservoir or swimming in the water.Visitors to Neyyar Dam and the wildlife sanctuary should be cautious around water bodies, especially if there are warning signs indicating the presence of crocodiles.
