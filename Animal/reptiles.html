Salt-Water Crocodile

The saltwater crocodile is a crocodilian native to saltwater habitats, brackish wetlands and freshwater rivers from India's east coast across Southeast Asia and the Sundaic region to northern Australia and Micronesia. It has been listed as Least Concern on the IUCN Red List since 1996.

Scientific Name:Crocodylus porosus
Habitat:Coastal brackish mangrove swamps,river deltas
Age:70 yrs
IUCN Status:Endangered

Bronze Headed Vine Snake

Ahaetulla perroteti, known commonly as the bronze-headed vine snake, Perrotet's vine snake, or the Western Ghats bronzeback, is a species of mildly venomous, rear-fanged snake in the family Colubridae. The species is endemic to the Western Ghats in South India.

Common Name:Perrotet's vine snake
Scientific Name:Ahaetulla perroteti
Natural Habitat:Grasslands
Age:12 yrs

Green-eyed bushfrog

Green-eyed bushfrogs only occur in disturbed sholas, a type of high-altitude evergreen forests which are found only in the southern portion of the Western Ghats. This species has also been found near the secondary forest and tea and eucalyptus plantations after very heavy rains, from about 1 m (3 ft 3 in) above the ground, from thickets of Lantana or leaves of Eupatorium glandulosum. Like other congeners, this species breeds by direct development.

Scientific Name:Raorchestes chlorosomma
Habitat:Evergreen forests
Regions:Kerala
IUCN Status:Endangered

WYNAD DAY GECKO

The Wynad day gecko (Cnemaspis wynadensis ) is a species of gecko. It is endemic to the Western Ghats in Kerala, India, and only known from two locations, Wayanad and Silent Valley National Park.

Scientific Name:Cnemaspis wynadensis
Habitat:Western Ghats in Kerala
Apperaence: Grows to 1.6 inches
IUCN Status:Endangered

Star Tortoise

The Indian star tortoise is a threatened tortoise species native to India, Pakistan and Sri Lanka where it inhabits dry areas and scrub forest. It has been listed as Vulnerable on the IUCN Red List since 2016, as the population is thought to comprise more than 10,000 individuals, but with a declining trend.

Scientific Name:Geochelone elegans
Habitat:Dry areas and Scrub forest
Age:35 to 80 yrs
IUCN Status:Endangered
