But both are different plants possessing pharmacologically similar properties. Brahmi is known for its utility in psychiatric and neurological conditions. It is used by Indian folklore as well as urban household for the children to improve the memory.     Characteristics of the Brahmi

    A small, water-loving perennial creeper of the Apiaceae family, brahmi/Bacopa Monnieri is thought to have originated in the Indian subcontinent, although it can be found in warm climates across the globe. Brahmi is the small creeping herb with the numerous branches. It grows to a height of 2 -3 feet and its branches are 10 -35 cm long. It has oval shaped leaves that are 1-2 cm long and 3- 8 mm broad. Leaves are formed in pairs along the stems. Small- tubular, five petaled flowers are white- purple in colour. Its stem is soft, succulent, and hairy with the glands. Roots emerge out of the nodules and directly go to the soil. The fruit is oval brahmiand sharp at apex Its slender stems support vibrant green leaves that are arguably this plant’s most distinctive feature. Many of its names are a reference to the appearance of its leaves. For instance, in ancient Sri Lanka, the gentle curling shape of the leaves was thought to resemble a cone, leading to the Singhalese name “gotu kola” or “cone leaf".

    Let`s Explore The Hidden Gems

    The leaves of the Brahmi plant contain active compounds, including bacosides, believed to contribute to its medicinal effects.

    The Brahmi plant (Bacopa monnieri) is a low-growing herb with small, succulent leaves.

    The Brahmi plant is a small herb with a low-lying and spreading growth habit.

    Brahmi is often used as a ground cover in gardens due to its spreading growth habit.

    Brahmi flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically white or light purple in color, and they contribute to the plant's ornamental appeal.

    The plant thrives in a humid and aquatic environment, commonly found in wet soil near water features.

    The Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve

    The Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve was the first biosphere reserve in India established in the year 1986. It is located in the Western Ghats and includes 2 of the 10 biogeographical provinces of India. Wide ranges of ecosystems and species diversity are found in this region. Thus, it was a natural choice for the premier biosphere reserve of the country. The Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve was established to conserve the rich biodiversity of the Western Ghats and protect several endangered species of flora and fauna.
