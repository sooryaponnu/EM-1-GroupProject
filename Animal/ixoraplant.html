Appearance

Ixora lawsonii is a compact shrub that typically grows to a height of 1-2 meters. Its growth habit is generally rounded and bushy, with multiple branches arising from the base. The leaves of Ixora lawsonii are glossy and dark green in color. They are arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The leaves are elliptical to lanceolate in shape, with pointed tips and smooth margins. They are relatively small compared to some other species of Ixora.The most striking feature of Ixora lawsonii is its flowers. The flowers are borne in dense clusters at the ends of branches, forming rounded inflorescences known as corymbs. Each individual flower has a tubular shape, with four spreading lobes. The color of the flowers ranges from bright orange to red-orange, and they often have a slightly tubular appearance. The vibrant coloration and dense clusters of flowers make Ixora lawsonii a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes. After flowering, Ixora lawsonii produces small, rounded fruits. These fruits are typically green when immature and may turn dark purple or black as they ripen. The fruits are not as showy as the flowers and are mainly of botanical interest rather than ornamental value. Overall, Ixora lawsonii is known for its attractive foliage and stunning clusters of bright orange to red-orange flowers, making it a prized addition to gardens and landscapes in regions where it is cultivated.

This region is known for its high levels of biodiversity and unique ecosystems. The Western Ghats are characterized by a range of habitats, including tropical evergreen forests, moist deciduous forests, and grasslands, where Ixora lawsonii can be found. Within the Western Ghats, Ixora lawsonii typically grows in the understory of evergreen and moist deciduous forests. It thrives in the shaded, moist conditions provided by the forest canopy. Ixora lawsonii prefers habitats with high humidity and moderate to dense shade. It is often found along stream banks, in ravines, and in other areas where moisture levels are relatively high. These conditions are typical of the Western Ghats' forested habitats. While Ixora lawsonii can be found at various altitudes within the Western Ghats, it is more commonly encountered at lower to mid elevations. Kanakakunnu Palace

Kanakakunnu Palace is a historic palace located in Thiruvananthapuram, the capital city of Kerala, India. It was originally built by the Travancore King Sree Moolam Thirunal in the late 19th century. It was constructed as a guest house for royal guests and dignitaries visiting the Travancore kingdom. The palace was later renovated and expanded during the reign of King Sree Chithira Thirunal. The palace is known for its exquisite architectural style, blending traditional Kerala architecture with elements of colonial and Dravidian styles. It features sloping roofs, wooden carvings,typical of Kerala's traditional architecture. The palace is surrounded by lush green gardens and offers panoramic views of the city.
