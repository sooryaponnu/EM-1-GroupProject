BLUE WINGED PARAKEET

Habitat

The Blue-winged Parakeet, or Malabar Parakeet, is endemic to the Western Ghats in southern India. Its habitat primarily includes the lush forests of the Western Ghats, extending from north of Bombay (around 19°N latitude) down to Kerala. This habitat also spans into the hills of the Palnis and Nilgiris as well as the adjoining Eastern Ghats, including the Biligirirangan range. The specific forests and hilly regions within this range provide a suitable environment for these parakeets, supporting their feeding and nesting requirements. For more detailed information, you can visit the Wikipedia page on the Blue-winged Parakeet.

Food

The Blue-winged Parakeet's diet mainly consists of seeds, fruits, and buds typical of parakeets. They forage in the forests of the Western Ghats, where they have access to a variety of plant-based food sources. These birds have also been observed feeding on the leaves of certain plants, such as Loranthus longiflorus. Their feeding habits are well-suited to the rich biodiversity of their habitat, allowing them to thrive in the dense forests and hilly regions of southern India. For more detailed information, you can visit the Wikipedia page on the Blue-winged Parakeet.

Nesting

Blue-winged Parakeets typically nest in tree holes, often utilizing cavities originally made by woodpeckers or barbets. They are known to breed after the northeast monsoon, with their chicks fledging before the southwest monsoon in June. These birds generally start breeding in December, and eggs are usually laid between December and January. The typical clutch size for Blue-winged Parakeets is four eggs, and it takes about 23 days for the eggs to hatch.

Breeding

The Blue-winged Parakeet breeds after the northeast monsoon, typically starting in December. They nest in tree holes, often utilizing cavities created by woodpeckers or barbets. The female lays a clutch of around four eggs, which hatch after approximately 23 days. During the initial brooding period, the male brings food to the female. As the chicks grow, the male takes a more active role in their care. The chicks fledge about a month after hatching and then leave the nest.

Lifespan and mortality

The specific lifespan and mortality rates of Blue-winged Parakeets in the wild are not well-documented in the available literature. However, like many parrot species, they likely have a relatively long lifespan, especially when compared to smaller bird species. Their mortality rates, particularly in the wild, can be influenced by factors such as predation, habitat loss, and disease. For captive birds, lifespan can be extended with proper care. For more detailed information, you might want to refer to ornithological studies or resources on parakeet species.

Behavior

The Blue-winged Parakeet exhibits typical parakeet behaviors that are adapted to its habitat in the Western Ghats. These birds are social and are often found in small flocks. They are agile flyers and are known to make rapid flights in forest clearings. Their diet consists of seeds, fruits, and buds, which they forage from the forest. During the breeding season, these parakeets become more vocal, using a series of screeching calls. They are also known to nest in tree holes, utilizing cavities made by other birds. This species' behavior reflects its adaptation to the forested regions of southern India. For more detailed information, you can visit the Wikipedia page on the Blue-winged Parakeet.

Chinnar Wildlife Sanctuary,Kerala

Chinnar Wildlife Sanctuary, located in Kerala's Idukki district, is a unique protected area within the Western Ghats, characterized by its dry deciduous forests and distinct ecological, floral, and geomorphological diversity, and is home to a variety of endangered and endemic species, including the vulnerable Grizzled Giant Squirrel and the Blue-winged Parakeet, while also hosting indigenous tribal communities like the Muthuvas and Pulayars, making it a fascinating destination for nature lovers, wildlife enthusiasts, and those interested in cultural experiences.

Aralam Wildlife Sanctuary,Kerala

Aralam Wildlife Sanctuary, located in the Kannur district of Kerala, is spread over an area of 55 square kilometers on the Western Ghats. Established in 1984, it is known for its diverse range of flora and fauna, including tropical and semi-evergreen forests. This sanctuary is the northernmost wildlife sanctuary of Kerala and offers a captivating experience with its serene environment and rich biodiversity.The sanctuary is home to various wildlife species such as elephants, gaur, sambar, spotted deer, barking deer, Nilgiri Langur, Hanuman Langur, and the Malabar Giant Squirrel. It's also notable for its large number of butterfly species, many of which are endemic to the Western Ghats. The sanctuary provides several trekking opportunities, including treks to Ambalappara and Meenmutty Falls.For those interested in visiting, the nearest railway station to Aralam Wildlife Sanctuary is Thalassery, which is about 55 kilometers away. The nearest airports are Kannur International Airport, located approximately 40 kilometers from the sanctuary, and Calicut International Airport, which is about 96 kilometers from Thalassery town.
